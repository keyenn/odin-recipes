LASAGNA

PRESENTATION

Lasagne alla Bolognese is an institution, typical for Sunday lunches. This rich and tasty dish originates from the Emilia region, specifically, from the city of Bologna. Lasagna, however, is known, appreciated, made, remade, tasted and shared not only throughout Italy, but also abroad as an Italian symbolic dish.

Layers of green egg pasta, dressed with the classic traditional ragù, béchamel and grated cheese. Obviously, lasagna has numerous and delicious variations such as the pumpkin and sausage, or the one with mushrooms, perfect for autumn menus. From the preparation to the ingredients, this recipe is the quintessence of the "richness" of traditional Bolognese cuisine, also famous for many other fresh pasta recipes and more. You just need to prepare it and enjoy it with your family for a very special day!

INGREDIENTS

INGREDIENTS FOR SPINACH EGG PASTA LAYERS
  • Remilled durum wheat semolina 2.1 cups (350 g)
  • Flour 00 1.3 cups (150 g)
  • Spinach 8.8 oz (250 g)
  • Eggs 2
  • Egg yolks 3
FOR THE BOLOGNESE SAUCE
  • Beef 10.6 oz (300 g) - (minced, coarsely ground and mixed)
  • Pancetta bacon 5.3 oz (150 g)
  • Carrots 0.5 cup (50 g)
  • Celery 0.5 cup (50 g)
  • Yellow onions 1 cup (50 g)
  • Red wine 0.5 cup (100 g)
  • Tomato puree 1.25 cup (300 g)
  • Vegetable broth to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil 1 tbsp
  • Fine salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
FOR THE BECHAMEL SAUCE
  • Butter 0.3 cup (70 g)
  • Flour 00 0.6 cup (70 g)
  • Whole milk 1 quart (1 l)
  • Fine salt 1 pinch
  • Nutmeg to taste
TO SEASON
  • Butter to taste
  • Parmigiano Reggiano DOP cheese 2.3 cups (270 g)

STEPS

  1. To prepare lasagne alla Bolognese, start with the meat sauce. Prepare the vegetable broth and ensure to keep it warm. Then take the pancetta bacon, cut it into strips first
  2. Chop it well
  3. Separately, finely chop the carrots, onions, celery and set aside
  4. In a saucepan, pour a drizzle of oil and the pancetta bacon. Use a ladle to spread it well
  5. Let it brown for a few minutes. Then add the chopped vegetables and cook for 5-6 minutes
  6. Add the minced meat. Stir and raise the heat. Let the meat brown slowly, it must be well browned to seal the juices and be soft.
  7. Deglaze with red wine
  8. Let it evaporate completely before adding the tomato puree
  9. The Bolognese sauce must cook for two hours. When it starts boiling again, you can add some hot broth, one or two ladles and let the sauce cook for another couple of hours at least.
  10. Let it cook with the lid on, without covering the pan completely
  11. Check and stir from time to time, add more broth when needed and let it cook. Now move on to the pasta. First, pour the spinach into a pan
  12. Add a little water, cover with a lid
  13. Cook until they are wilted, it will take 5-6 minutes in total
  14. Drain them, let them cool and squeeze them well
  15. Transfer the spinach to a mixer
  16. Blend them until you get a puree
  17. You will need to get approx 3.5 cups (100 g) of it. Now on a pastry board pour the semolina and 00 flour
  18. Add the spinach and create a fountain shape. Add the lightly beaten eggs and egg yolks
  19. Begin to knead everything starting from the center
  20. Knead well until you get a homogeneous dough
  21. Wrap it in plastic wrap
  22. Let it rest for 30 minutes at room temperature. In the meantime, prepare the béchamel sauce. Heat the milk in a saucepan without boiling it.
  23. In another pan, place the butter and let it melt
  24. Add the flour in one go
  25. Cook for a few minutes until you get a golden roux
  26. Pour the hot milk in three times, mixing well
  27. Add salt and flavor with nutmeg
  28. Continue to mix until you get a creamy béchamel
  29. Transfer to a small bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside. As soon as 30 minutes have elapsed, take the dough back and take a piece
  30. Cover the rest of the dough with plastic wrap to keep it from drying out.
  31. With the help of a little semolina flatten it slightly on the pastry board to form a rectangle and roll out the dough in the machine
  32. Start from a wider thickness, then fold the dough and pass it over. When you feel it is too wet, sprinkle with a little semolina. Gradually reduce the thickness until you get a sheet just over 0.2 inches (0.5 mm) thick. Transfer it to a pastry board and cut into large rectangles 12x8 inches (30x20 cm)
  33. Continue this way until the dough is finished and in the meantime put a pot of water on the stove and add salt. When it starts to boil, immerse one sheet at a time
  34. Wait 30-40 seconds and using a skimmer drain it
  35. Transfer it to a tray with a cloth
  36. Dab to remove excess water. Continue this way, placing the cooked sheets side by side, without overlapping them. As soon as the Bolognese sauce is ready
  37. Season with salt and pepper and proceed to the composition of the lasagna.
  38. Take a large 12x8 inches (30x20 cm) lasagna pan and grease the bottom
  39. Add a thin layer of béchamel and one of Bolognese sauce
  40. Place the first sheet
  41. Add a layer of béchamel and a layer of meat sauce
  42. Add grated Parmesan cheese
  43. Place another sheet of pasta on top
  44. Add another layer of béchamel and one of meat sauce
  45. Add grated cheese
  46. Continue like this until you have 5 layers. After placing the last sheet, add the Bolognese sauce in order to completely cover the pasta sheet
  47. Sprinkle with grated cheese, add some tufts of butter
  48. Bake in a preheated static oven at 338°F (170°C) for 40 minutes
